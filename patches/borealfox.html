welcome to borealfox, heres some stuff you may want to configure (you can reopen this page by running "borealfox-settings" in your terminal)

security and privacy

force https[?]blocks all plain http connections and upgrades them to https. sites without https support will fail to load.
fingerprinting protection[?]standard blocks known scripts. aggressive (tor-level) spoofs hardware but breaks some sites.
enable letterboxing[?]forces the page viewport to generic dimensions by adding gray borders, preventing trackers from fingerprinting your exact screen size.
first-party isolation[?]strictly isolates cookies and cache to the domain in the url bar, preventing cross-site tracking.
global privacy control[?]sends a gpc signal to every site telling them not to sell or share your data. legally binding in some jurisdictions.
disable webrtc[?]webrtc can expose your real ip address even through a vpn. disabling it prevents this leak. breaks video calling apps like google meet.
disable drm[?]removes widevine and eme. drm is proprietary software with no auditable source code running in your browser.
disable webgl[?]removes hardware 3d graphics access. highly fingerprintable. breaks complex browser games and some 3d map sites.
dns over https[?]encrypts your dns queries and routes them through a trusted provider.
tracking protection
social tracking protection[?]blocks facebook, twitter and other social media trackers embedded on third-party sites.
block cryptomining[?]prevents websites from using your cpu to mine cryptocurrency without your knowledge.
bounce tracking protection[?]detects and clears state from redirect-based tracking where you are briefly sent through a tracking domain before reaching your destination.
strip tracking parameters from urls[?]removes utm_ and other tracking parameters appended to urls when you click links.
clear selected data on close[?]automatically wipes selected data every time you close firefox.

disable search and form history[?]stops firefox from remembering what you type into search bars and web forms.
disable search suggestions[?]stops firefox from sending every keystroke in the url bar to your search engine.

media and ui

block autoplay
disable pdf scripting[?]prevents javascript embedded in pdf files from executing when opened in the browser.
disable smooth scrolling[?]disables modern scroll animation. page jumps instantly like it did in 1999. highly responsive.
use userchrome.css ui[?]applies the borealfox custom chrome stylesheet. gives tabs a flat square look with a colored top border on the active tab.
selected tab color

network

disable ipv6[?]forces the browser to use legacy ipv4. can prevent routing leaks if your vpn does not handle ipv6 correctly.
disable dns prefetch[?]stops firefox from resolving dns for links on a page before you click them. reduces latency but leaks which links you might click.
disable speculative connections[?]stops firefox from opening tcp connections to servers before you actually click a link.
disable link prefetch
disable captive portal detection[?]stops firefox from pinging mozilla servers to check if you are behind a captive portal (hotel/airport wifi login pages).
show punycode domains[?]shows the raw punycode form of internationalized domain names (e.g. xn--). helps spot homograph phishing attacks.

telemetry and bloat

disable telemetry
disable normandy studies[?]normandy is mozilla's remote experimentation system. it can push studies and feature changes to your browser without your explicit consent.
disable pocket
disable firefox accounts
disable ai features
disable safe browsing[?]safe browsing sends url hashes to google to check for malware. disabling it improves privacy but removes that layer of protection.
disable crash reports
disable service workers[?]prevents sites from installing background workers. blocks push notifications and offline web apps.
